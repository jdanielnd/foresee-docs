Goals x Forecasts in Sales

It is common for companies to use sales estimates compiled by their vendors in their decision-making processes, or even to consider the goals set by the company themselves as the sales estimate.

These estimates, although easier to obtain, usually present a problem: they are not impartial enough.

Sales estimates generally show a conservative bias, while the goals set by the company are usually characterized by a more motivational bias. Therefore, larger companies use statistical projections in their decision-making processes, because these are objective and unbiased.

What is "most likely"?

Constructed from probability methods, statistical projections indicate the most likely scenario for sales within a particular period. This allows for a clear separation between what is a goal and what is a projection.

Statistical projections reduce estimation errors

Goals and forecasts are fundamental to the management of any company. But it is important to know when to use one or the other.

For making decisions, it is important to consider the more likely scenario as a sales forecast. And that is exactly what statistical projections provide.

By using statistical projections, the company reduces its estimation errors, which consistently improves the quality of their decisions, and therefore their results.
